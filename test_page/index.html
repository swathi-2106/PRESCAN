Cosmic Facts

🌌

The Milky Way

Our galaxy contains between 100-400 billion stars and is about 105,000 light-years in diameter. We're located about 26,000 light-years from the galactic center.

⚫

Black Holes

The supermassive black hole at the center of our galaxy, Sagittarius A*, has a mass of about 4 million times that of our Sun, yet it's incredibly compact.

🌟

Stellar Birth

Stars are born in stellar nurseries called nebulae, where clouds of gas and dust collapse under gravity, heating up until nuclear fusion ignites.

🪐

Saturn's Rings

Saturn's magnificent rings are made of billions of particles of ice and rock, ranging in size from tiny grains to house-sized chunks.

🌙

Moon Mysteries

The Moon is gradually moving away from Earth at a rate of about 3.8 centimeters per year, a process that will continue for billions of years.

☄️

Cosmic Speed

The fastest man-made object, the Parker Solar Probe, travels at speeds up to 430,000 mph, fast enough to travel from New York to Tokyo in under a minute.
